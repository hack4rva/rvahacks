# Brand Guidelines

**Last Updated:** December 2024

---

## Brand Overview

**Hack for RVA** is Richmond's first civic hackathon — a community event bringing together technologists, designers, and civic-minded residents to build real solutions for our city.

---

## Event Name

**Primary:** Hack for RVA
**Full Name:** Hack for RVA: Richmond Civic Hackathon
**Hashtag:** #HackForRVA

Do not use:
- ❌ HackRVA (different organization)
- ❌ RVA Hacks (outdated)
- ❌ Richmond Hackathon (too generic)

## Typography

### Headlines
- **Font:** Inter Bold (or similar modern sans-serif)
- **Weights:** 700 (Bold), 600 (Semi-bold)
- **Sizes:**
  - H1: 48-60px
  - H2: 36-42px
  - H3: 24-30px
  - H4: 18-24px

### Body Text
- **Font:** Inter Regular
- **Weight:** 400 (Regular)
- **Size:** 16-18px
- **Line Height:** 1.5-1.6

### Monospace (Code)
- **Font:** JetBrains Mono, Fira Code, or system mono
- **Use:** Code examples, technical content

## Logo Usage

### Primary Logo
- Full "Hack for RVA" wordmark

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice

**Inclusive**
- Everyone is welcome
- No experience required
- Diverse perspectives valued

**Action-Oriented**
- Focus on building and doing
- "Build" not "learn about building"
- "Ship" not "explore"

**Civic-Minded**
- About Richmond, for Richmond
- Real problems, real impact
- Community over competition

**Optimistic**
- Solutions over problems
- Possibility over limitations
- Celebration over criticism

### Tone Examples

✅ **Do say:**
- "Build something that matters"
- "48 hours to ship a real solution"
- "Everyone has something to contribute"

❌ **Don't say:**
- "Change the world" (too vague)
- "Hack the planet" (wrong connotation)
- "Only for coders" (exclusionary)

## Photography Guidelines

### Subject Matter
- Diverse groups collaborating
- Richmond landmarks and neighborhoods
- Technology and creativity in action
- Community events and gatherings

### Style
- Natural lighting preferred
- Authentic moments over posed shots
- Inclusive representation
- Action and energy

### Treatment
- Team headshots: consistent blue background
- Event photos: minimal filters
- Social media: can be more casual
