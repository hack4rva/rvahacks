# Role Cards: Support Volunteers

## Overview

Support volunteers help teams directly during the hacking period. These roles require specific expertise and can be done in flexible time blocks.

**Total Support Volunteers Needed: 30-40 people**

---

## Technical Mentor

### Role Card

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | `technical-mentor` |
| **Category** | Support |
| **Location** | Virtual (Discord) or in-person at satellite sites |
| **Shift** | 2-4 hour shifts (flexible) |
| **Quantity** | 8-12 |
| **Reports To** | Hacker Space Lead |
| **Training Required** | None (expertise assumed) |

### You Own

- Respond to mentor queue requests in your specialty areas
- Join team video calls or visit in person to help
- Provide guidance without taking over the keyboard
- Help teams make good architecture decisions for 48-hour scope
- Suggest appropriate tools and technologies
- Review code and suggest improvements
- Help debug tricky issues
- Encourage teams and build confidence

### You Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Channel |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Team needs help outside your expertise | Mentor Coordinator | Discord #mentor-queue |
| Team seems stuck or demoralized | Volunteer Coordinator | Discord DM |
| Potential cheating or rule violation | Event Manager | Slack DM |
| Request for resources not available (cloud credits, etc.) | Technology Support Coordinator | Discord |
| Team needs subject matter context | Data Ambassador or SME | Discord |

### Decision Authority

- Spend up to 30 minutes with a single team
- Recommend specific technologies or approaches
- Suggest teams pivot or narrow scope
- Connect teams with other mentors you know
- Decide when you've helped enough and team should try independently

### Success Criteria

- Teams you help make meaningful progress
- Teams feel unblocked and encouraged
- Teams learn something (not just given answers)
- Positive feedback from teams

### Specialty Areas (indicate yours)

- [ ] Frontend (React, Vue, etc.)
- [ ] Backend (Node, Python, etc.)
- [ ] Data Science / ML
- [ ] Mobile (iOS, Android, React Native)
- [ ] Cloud / DevOps (AWS, GCP, Azure)
- [ ] Database (SQL, NoSQL)
- [ ] APIs / Integrations
- [ ] No-code / Low-code (Bubble, Retool, etc.)
- [ ] GIS / Mapping
- [ ] AI / LLMs

### Skills Required

- Strong technical skills in at least one area
- Teaching ability (explain concepts clearly)
- Patience with stressed teams
- Comfort with video calls

### Skills Preferred

- Full-stack experience
- Hackathon experience (participant or mentor)
- Civic tech or open data experience
- Multiple programming languages

### Mentoring Best Practices

1. **Ask before coding** — Understand the problem before suggesting solutions
2. **Teach, don't do** — Guide them to the answer rather than typing it yourself
3. **Validate the approach** — Help them confirm they're solving the right problem
4. **Scope check** — Is this achievable in remaining time?
5. **Encourage** — Hackathons are stressful; be positive
6. **Time-box** — 20-30 min max, then let them apply what you taught

- Hackathon experience
- Civic tech or social impact design
- Video/motion design skills

### Design Mentoring Tips

1. **Start with the user** — Who is this for? What problem are they solving?
2. **Focus on core flow** — What's the one thing users need to do?
3. **Prototype fast** — Paper sketches are fine for hackathons
4. **Accessibility basics** — Color contrast, font size, keyboard navigation
5. **Demo-ready** — What will look good on stage in 3 minutes?
6. **Don't over-polish** — Good enough > perfect for hackathons

## Pitch Coach

### Role Card

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | `pitch-coach` |
| **Category** | Support |
| **Location** | Virtual (Discord) or in-person |
| **Shift** | Saturday evening + Sunday morning |
| **Quantity** | 4-6 |
| **Reports To** | Business Room Lead |
| **Training Required** | None (expertise assumed) |

### You Own

- Run pitch practice sessions (group or 1:1)
- Help teams structure their 3-5 minute story
- Coach on presentation delivery (pace, energy, clarity)
- Advise on demo flow and what to show
- Provide feedback on slide design if applicable
- Help teams anticipate judge questions
- Build presenter confidence

### You Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Channel |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Team has no working demo | Technical Mentor | Discord #hacker-space |
| Team conflict affecting pitch prep | Volunteer Coordinator | Slack DM |
| Team needs subject matter input | Data Ambassador or SME | Discord |
| Team severely underprepared | Business Room Lead | Discord DM |

### Decision Authority

- Practice session format and structure
- Direct feedback on content and delivery
- Recommend teams cut or add specific content
- Pair teams for peer feedback
- How to prioritize limited practice time

### Success Criteria

- Teams feel confident going into pitches
- Teams stay within time limits
- Pitches tell a clear, compelling story
- Positive feedback from teams

### Pitch Coaching Areas

- [ ] Story structure and narrative arc
- [ ] Public speaking and delivery
- [ ] Demo strategy and flow
- [ ] Slide design and visual aids
- [ ] Q&A preparation
- [ ] Handling nerves and stage fright

### Skills Required

- Presentation skills
- Storytelling ability
- Constructive feedback delivery
- Patience with nervous presenters

### Skills Preferred

- Startup pitch experience
- Public speaking coaching
- Previous hackathon judging
- Demo Day / investor pitch experience

### Pitch Coaching Framework

**3-Minute Pitch Structure:**
1. **Hook (15 sec)** — Why should judges care?
2. **Problem (30 sec)** — What's broken? Who's affected?
3. **Solution (60 sec)** — What did you build? Show demo.
4. **Impact (30 sec)** — What changes if this works?
5. **Ask (15 sec)** — What do you need to make it real?
6. **Close (10 sec)** — Memorable ending

**Common Pitfalls to Catch:**
- Too much background, not enough demo
- Solution before problem is clear
- No clear ask or next step
- Technical jargon for non-technical judges
- Running over time

## First Aid Volunteer

### Role Card

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | `first-aid-volunteer` |
| **Category** | Support |
| **Location** | On-site (Science Museum or satellite) |
| **Shift** | On-site shifts as scheduled |
| **Quantity** | 3-4 |
| **Reports To** | Event Manager |
| **Training Required** | Current CPR/First Aid certification |

### You Own

- Be available and visible during your shift
- Respond to minor health issues (headaches, small cuts, etc.)
- Know location of first aid supplies
- Monitor for signs of exhaustion or stress
- Perform wellness checks if requested
- Know when and how to call 911

### You Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Channel |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Any emergency requiring 911 | 911 + Event Manager | Phone immediately |
| Serious health concern | Event Manager | Phone/radio |
| Mental health crisis | Event Manager | Phone/radio |
| First aid supplies depleted | Site Captain or Event Manager | In person |

### Decision Authority

- How to handle minor injuries
- Whether to recommend participant take a break
- When situation requires professional medical attention

### Success Criteria

- All minor incidents handled appropriately
- Serious issues escalated quickly
- No health emergencies mishandled
- Participants feel safe

### Skills Required

- Current CPR/First Aid certification
- Calm under pressure
- Good judgment about severity
- Clear communication

### Skills Preferred

- EMT or medical background
- Previous event first aid experience
- Mental health first aid training
