# Role Cards: Pillar Stakeholders

## Overview

Each of the 7 pillars has three stakeholder roles:
1. City Liaison
2. Nonprofit Partner (organization + envoy)
3. Corporate Sponsor

These are **recruited partnerships**, not traditional volunteer roles. They require targeted outreach and relationship management.

---

## City Liaison

### Role Card

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | `city-liaison` |
| **Category** | Pillar Stakeholder |
| **Location** | Science Museum (Friday/Sunday) |
| **Quantity** | 7 needed (4-7 people if covering multiple pillars) |
| **Commitment** | Friday kickoff (2-3 hrs) + on-call during hacking + optional Sunday |
| **Reports To** | Event Manager |

### You Own

- Represent the City's perspective on your pillar's challenges
- Present at Friday kickoff session (15-20 min + Q&A)
- Share what data and resources the City has available
- Explain existing city initiatives in this space
- Discuss implementation pathways for winning solutions
- Be available for team questions via Discord (as time permits)
- Champion adopted solutions post-event

### You Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Channel |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Media inquiries about city position | Mayor's Office Communications | Email/phone |
| Questions about budget or commitments | Event Manager | Slack DM |
| Data access requests beyond your authority | City Data Liaison | Discord |
| Conflict with nonprofit or corporate partner | Event Manager | In person |

### Decision Authority

- Share publicly available city information and data
- Discuss general implementation possibilities
- Connect teams with relevant city staff (within reason)
- Agree to post-event follow-up meetings with winning teams

### Success Criteria

- Teams leave Friday understanding the city's perspective
- At least 3 teams reference city data/priorities in their submissions
- Winning solution has clear implementation pathway discussed
- Post-event handoff meeting scheduled within 2 weeks

### Skills Required

- Subject matter expertise in pillar domain
- Public speaking comfort
- Authority to discuss implementation possibilities
- Familiarity with city data and resources

### Skills Preferred

- Previous hackathon or tech event experience
- Connections to relevant nonprofits
- Experience with civic tech or open data

### Notes

- The same person may cover 2-3 related pillars
- Must have department head approval to participate
- Should be comfortable with informal Q&A from teams

## Nonprofit Partner

### Role Card

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | `nonprofit-partner` |
| **Category** | Pillar Stakeholder |
| **Location** | Science Museum (Friday + Sunday) |
| **Quantity** | 7 organizations (1 per pillar) |
| **Commitment** | Friday speaker (15-20 min + Q&A) + Sunday judging (3-4 hrs) |
| **Reports To** | Nonprofit Partnership Coordinator |

### Who

The **Nonprofit Partner** is an organization. Their **Envoy** (typically Exec Director or senior leader) is the active participant.

### You Own (Envoy)

- Present on-the-ground reality of the issue at Friday kickoff
- Explain what solutions exist and what gaps remain
- Share stories that humanize the data
- Serve as judge for your pillar's award on Sunday
- Evaluate submissions against real-world feasibility
- Consider adopting winning solutions for your organization

### You Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Channel |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Conflict of interest with a submission | Judge Coordinator | In person, discreetly |
| Questions about judging criteria | Judge Coordinator | Before judging begins |
| Request for ongoing involvement post-event | Nonprofit Partnership Coordinator | Email |
| Concerns about a team's approach | Data Ambassador | Discord |

### Decision Authority

- Score submissions based on your expertise and judgment
- Recommend solutions for adoption by your organization
- Offer to mentor winning teams post-event
- Share your organization's resources and connections

### Success Criteria

- Friday presentation sparks at least 5 team questions
- Judging completed on time with thoughtful feedback
- At least 1 submission could realistically help your organization
- Connection made with winning team for potential adoption

### Benefits to Organization

- Visibility in Richmond tech/civic community
- First look at innovative solutions to your challenges
- Networking with corporate sponsors and city leaders
- Potential to adopt winning solutions (open source = free)
- Recruiting visibility with skilled volunteers

### Skills Required (Envoy)

- Deep knowledge of on-the-ground challenges
- Public speaking and storytelling ability
- Judgment about solution feasibility
- Authority to discuss organizational adoption

### Skills Preferred

- Previous judging or evaluation experience
- Technical literacy (enough to understand solutions)
- Connections to other community organizations

### Notes

- Organization provides envoy's time as in-kind contribution
- No financial commitment required
- Open source license means free adoption of solutions

## Corporate Sponsor

### Role Card

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | `corporate-sponsor` |
| **Category** | Pillar Stakeholder |
| **Location** | Science Museum (Friday) |
| **Quantity** | 7 companies (1 per pillar) |
| **Commitment** | Friday kickoff presence (5-10 min) + $1K award funding |
| **Reports To** | Sponsorship Lead |

### You Own

- Share why your company cares about this pillar's issues
- Present business perspective at Friday kickoff (5-10 min)
- Fund the $1,000 pillar award
- Network with city and nonprofit leaders
- Optional: provide mentors from your company
- Optional: participate in judging

### You Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Channel |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Award payment logistics | Sponsorship Lead | Email |
| Branding or logo questions | Sponsorship Lead | Email |
| Media inquiries | Social Media Manager | Slack |
| Mentor volunteer coordination | Mentor Coordinator | Discord |

### Decision Authority

- How to frame your company's interest in the pillar
- Whether to provide mentors from your company
- Whether to participate in judging
- Post-event engagement with winning teams

### Success Criteria

- Your perspective adds value to Friday session
- Award funding received before event
- Positive networking with city and nonprofit leaders
- Your logo visible on pillar materials
- At least 1 meaningful connection made

### Benefits

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Branding** | Logo on all pillar-related materials, website, and signage |
| **Networking** | Direct access to City and nonprofit leaders at your table |
| **Judge Seat** | Optional participation in judging your pillar |
| **Recruiting** | Visibility with 200+ technologists, designers, strategists |
| **Alignment** | Public alignment with Mayor's priorities |
| **CSR** | Documented community investment for CSR reporting |

### Sponsorship Package

- **Minimum:** $1,000 (award funding)
- **Includes:** Logo placement, Friday speaker slot, networking access
- **Optional add-ons:** Judge seat, mentor coordination, swag inclusion

### Skills Required (Company Representative)

- Ability to speak to company's interest in civic issues
- Comfort with public speaking
- Authority to represent company at community events

### Skills Preferred

- Previous community engagement experience
- Connections to tech talent
- Understanding of civic tech landscape

### Notes

- Financial commitment goes to Fiscal Sponsor
- Company representative should arrive 30 min before Friday session
- Consider sending 2-3 people for better networking coverage

## Pillar Stakeholder Coordination

### Friday Session Format (per pillar)

| Time | Activity | Speaker |
|------|----------|---------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Introduction | Room Captain |
| 0:05-0:20 | City Perspective | City Liaison |
| 0:20-0:40 | On-the-Ground Reality | Nonprofit Envoy |
| 0:40-0:50 | Business Perspective | Corporate Sponsor |
| 0:50-1:15 | Panel Q&A | All three |
| 1:15-1:30 | Team Discussion/Formation | Facilitated |

### Sunday Judging (per pillar)

| Time | Activity | Participants |
|------|----------|--------------|
| Morning | Virtual first-round review | Nonprofit Envoy + optional judges |
| Afternoon | Live finalist pitches | Nonprofit Envoy + City Liaison (optional) |
| Before ceremony | Final deliberation | All pillar judges |

### Communication Cadence

| Timing | Communication | Owner |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| 12 weeks before | Initial outreach | Respective coordinator |
| 8 weeks before | Confirmation and logistics | Respective coordinator |
| 4 weeks before | Speaker prep materials | Friday Programming Lead |
| 2 weeks before | Orientation call (all stakeholders together) | Event Manager |
| 1 week before | Final logistics and run-of-show | Event Manager |
| Day before | Reminder and emergency contacts | Event Manager |
