# Role Cards: Pre-Event Volunteers

## Overview

Pre-event volunteers help prepare for Hack for RVA in the 6-8 weeks before the event. These roles have flexible, distributed time commitments.

**Total Pre-Event Volunteers Needed: 25-35 people**

---

## Outreach Ambassador

### Role Card

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | `outreach-ambassador` |
| **Category** | Pre-Event |
| **Location** | Various (campuses, meetups, community events) |
| **Quantity** | 8-10 |
| **Commitment** | 2-4 hrs/week for 6 weeks |
| **Reports To** | Social Media Manager |
| **Training Required** | 30-min orientation |

### You Own

- Recruit participants at universities, meetups, and community groups
- Present about Hack for RVA at assigned events
- Distribute promotional materials (flyers, stickers)
- Staff information tables at campus events
- Share social media content within your networks
- Track outreach results (events attended, estimated reach)
- Report back on what messaging resonates

### You Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Channel |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Request for official partnership | Sponsorship Lead | Slack |
| Media inquiry | Social Media Manager | Slack immediately |
| Question about event details you can't answer | Registration Manager | Slack |
| Large group interested in attending together | Volunteer Coordinator | Slack |

### Decision Authority

- Choose which events to attend within your assigned area
- Adapt messaging for different audiences
- Give away promotional items as you see fit
- Schedule your own outreach activities

### Success Criteria

- Attend at least 4 outreach events over 6 weeks
- Reach at least 100 potential participants
- At least 10 registrations attributed to your outreach
- Report back on messaging effectiveness

### Skills Required

- Comfortable public speaking to small groups
- Enthusiastic about civic tech and Richmond
- Reliable and self-directed

### Skills Preferred

- Existing connections to target communities
- Previous event promotion experience
- Active social media presence

## Venue Scout

### Role Card

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | `venue-scout` |
| **Category** | Pre-Event |
| **Location** | Various satellite venues |
| **Quantity** | 5-8 |
| **Commitment** | 2-3 hours per venue |
| **Reports To** | Satellite Location Coordinator |
| **Training Required** | 15-min checklist review |

### You Own

- Visit assigned satellite venue(s)
- Document WiFi speed and reliability (run speed tests)
- Count available outlets and power access
- Assess workspace capacity (seats, tables)
- Photograph space for documentation
- Note accessibility features (ADA, parking, transit)
- Complete venue assessment checklist
- Identify any concerns or issues

### You Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Channel |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Venue contact unresponsive | Satellite Location Coordinator | Slack |
| Major infrastructure concerns | Satellite Location Coordinator | Slack |
| Safety issues | Event Manager | Slack immediately |
| Venue wants to back out | Satellite Location Coordinator | Slack |

### Decision Authority

- When to schedule venue visits
- How to document findings
- Whether to suggest venue improvements

### Success Criteria

- All assigned venues visited and assessed
- Checklist completed with photos
- Report submitted at least 3 weeks before event
- Any concerns flagged promptly

### Venue Assessment Checklist

- [ ] WiFi speed test (minimum 25 Mbps down)
- [ ] WiFi guest access confirmed
- [ ] Power outlet count and locations
- [ ] Seating capacity
- [ ] Table/workspace availability
- [ ] Accessibility (wheelchair, elevators)
- [ ] Parking availability
- [ ] Transit access
- [ ] Restroom availability
- [ ] Food/drink policy
- [ ] After-hours access policy
- [ ] Contact person identified
- [ ] Photos taken

### Skills Required

- Reliable transportation
- Attention to detail
- Basic technical literacy (WiFi testing)

### Skills Preferred

- Event planning experience
- Local Richmond knowledge
- Photography skills
