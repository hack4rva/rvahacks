# Pillar Ecosystem: Cross-Sector Partnerships

## Overview

Each of the 7 pillars in Mayor Avula's Action Plan has a complete stakeholder ecosystem consisting of:

1. **City Liaison** â€” Government representative who champions the pillar
2. **Nonprofit Partner** â€” Organization working on-the-ground in this domain

Additionally, **1 Data Ambassador** serves across all pillars as a cross-cutting resource.

---

## The Magic Moment

The pillar ecosystem creates a unique convening opportunity:

**Friday Kickoff per Pillar:**
- City Liaison presents: "Here's what government sees"
- Nonprofit Envoy presents: "Here's what's happening on the ground"
- Corporate Sponsor presents: "Here's why business cares"

This brings City + NGO + Business leaders together at the same table, discussing the same civic challenge. That cross-sector dialogue is as valuable as the hackathon itself.

**Sunday Judging:**
- Nonprofit envoys judge their pillar awards
- Creates accountability: the people who know the problem best evaluate solutions

## Stakeholder Role Definitions

### City Liaison

**Who:** City staff from relevant department (may cover multiple pillars due to headcount constraints)

**Commitment:**
- Friday kickoff session (2-3 hours)
- Available for team questions during hacking (optional, via Discord)
- Sunday judging (optional)

**Responsibilities:**
- Present government perspective on pillar challenges
- Explain what data/resources the city has available
- Discuss implementation pathways for winning solutions
- Champion adopted solutions post-event

**Recruitment Owner:** Event Manager / City partnership lead

**Notes:**
- The same city person may cover 2-3 related pillars
- Must have authority to speak to implementation possibilities
- Should be comfortable with public speaking

---

### Nonprofit Partner

**Who:** Organization working in the pillar domain. Their envoy (typically Exec Director or senior leader) serves as the active participant.

**Commitment:**
- Friday kickoff speaker (15-20 min presentation + Q&A)
- Sunday judging (3-4 hours) â€” serves as judge for their pillar award
- Available for team questions during hacking (via Discord, as available)

**Responsibilities:**
- Speak about on-the-ground reality of the issue
- Explain what solutions already exist and what gaps remain
- Judge submissions for their pillar award
- Consider adopting winning solutions

**Benefits:**
- Visibility in Richmond tech/civic community
- First look at innovative solutions to their challenges
- Networking with corporate sponsors and city leaders
- Potential to adopt winning solutions at no cost (open source)

**Recruitment Owner:** Nonprofit Partnership Coordinator

### Corporate Sponsor

**Who:** Company with interest in the pillar domain, willing to fund the award and participate.

**Commitment:**
- Friday kickoff presence (share business perspective, 5-10 min)
- Funding ($1,000 for pillar award)
- Optional: provide mentors, judge participation

**Responsibilities:**
- Share why their company cares about this issue
- Fund the $1,000 pillar award
- Network with city and nonprofit leaders

**Benefits:**
- Branding on all pillar-related materials
- Networking with City + NGO leaders at the table
- Judge seat (optional)
- Recruiting visibility with hackathon participants
- Alignment with Mayor's priorities

**Sponsorship Package Value:** $1,000 minimum (award funding)

**Recruitment Owner:** Sponsorship Lead

|--------|------------------------------|

## Cross-Cutting Role: Data Ambassador

**Who:** Civic data generalist with broad Richmond knowledge

**Commitment:**
- Friday orientation (1 hour) â€” meet pillar stakeholders, understand data landscape
- Saturday on-call (6-8 hours) â€” available via Discord to help teams

**Responsibilities:**
- Help teams understand data across all 7 pillars
- Explain what datasets exist and their limitations
- Prevent teams from solving wrong problems
- Point teams to relevant datasets and APIs
- Coordinate with City Data Liaison for data access questions

**Skills Required:**
- Broad civic data knowledge
- Teaching ability
- Richmond-specific expertise
- Familiarity with common data sources (Census, Open Data Portal, GRTC, etc.)

**Recruitment Owner:** City Data Liaison
