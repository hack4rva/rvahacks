# Code of Conduct

**Last Updated:** December 2024

---

## Our Commitment

Hack for RVA is dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, political views, previous hackathon attendance or level of experience, or technology choices.

We do not tolerate harassment of event participants in any form.

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants, sponsors, volunteers, speakers, judges, and organizers are expected to:

- **Be respectful and inclusive** in all interactions
- **Collaborate constructively** with others
- **Help create a welcoming environment** for newcomers
- **Use welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Be mindful of your surroundings** and fellow participants
- **Alert organizers** if you notice a dangerous situation or someone in distress
- **Follow all event rules and instructions** from organizers and venue staff

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered harassment and are unacceptable:

- **Verbal harassment** including offensive comments related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, or technology choices
- **Sexual harassment** including unwelcome sexual attention, inappropriate physical contact, and sexual imagery in public spaces
- **Intimidation** including stalking, following, or threatening photography/recording
- **Sustained disruption** of talks, workshops, or other events
- **Inappropriate physical contact** without consent
- **Unwelcome attention** after being asked to stop
- **Advocating for or encouraging** any of the above behavior

---

## Consequences

Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately.

If a participant engages in harassing behavior, event organizers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

- Warning the offender
- Expelling the offender from the event without a refund
- Banning the offender from future events
- Reporting the behavior to appropriate authorities

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness harassment, or have any other concerns, please report it immediately:

### During the Event
- Find any volunteer or organizer (they'll have identifiable badges/shirts)
- Go to the Help Desk
- Use the anonymous reporting form (link TBD)

### Before or After the Event
- Email: conduct@hackforrva.org (TBD)
- Anonymous form: [Link TBD]

### What Happens When You Report
1. You'll be taken seriously
2. You can remain anonymous if you prefer
3. We'll investigate the situation
4. We'll take appropriate action
5. We'll follow up with you (if you've shared contact info)

---

## Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. We respect the privacy of those involved in any report.

---

## Photography & Recording

- **Official photography:** Event photographers will be present; by attending, you consent to being photographed for event documentation
- **Personal photography:** Ask permission before photographing or recording other participants
- **Opt-out:** If you don't want to be photographed, let a volunteer know and we'll provide a visual indicator

---

## Alcohol & Substances

- This is a family-friendly event
- No alcohol is permitted
- Illegal substances are prohibited
- If you need prescription medication, bring it in original packaging

---

## Weapons

No weapons of any kind are permitted at the event.

---

## Food Allergies

Please indicate any food allergies during registration. We cannot guarantee a completely allergen-free environment, but we'll do our best to accommodate dietary needs.

---

## Medical Emergencies

- Alert any volunteer or organizer immediately
- First aid supplies are available at the Help Desk
- AED locations will be announced at opening ceremony
- For serious emergencies, call 911

---

## This Code of Conduct Applies To

- All event venues (Science Museum and satellite locations)
- Online spaces (Discord, Devpost, social media)
- Event-related social gatherings
- All participants, sponsors, speakers, volunteers, and organizers
