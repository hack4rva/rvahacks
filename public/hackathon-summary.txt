HACK FOR RVA - RVA CIVIC HACKATHON SUMMARY
============================================

Hack for RVA is a 48-hour civic technology hackathon taking place 
March 27–29, 2026 at the Science Museum of Virginia in Richmond.

THE BIG PICTURE
---------------
This isn't just another hackathon—it's designed for real civic impact. 
Winning solutions will be adopted by City of Richmond departments, 
with legal pathways pre-cleared for open source licensing.

KEY DETAILS
-----------
• Duration: 48 hours
• Prize Pool: $15,000+
• Tracks: 7
• When: March 27–29, 2026
• Where: Science Museum of Virginia

THE 7 TRACKS (Aligned with Mayor Avula's Action Plan)
------------------------------------------------------
1. Thriving City Hall – Government efficiency, IT modernization, open data
2. Thriving Neighborhoods – Housing, affordability, eviction prevention
3. Thriving Families – K-12 education, youth programs, family support
4. Thriving Economy – Workforce development, small business, economic mobility
5. Inclusive Communities – Health equity, civil rights, social services access
6. Thriving Environment – Climate action, sustainability, environmental justice
7. City Stories – Historical truth-telling, cultural preservation, racial reconciliation

WHAT MAKES IT DIFFERENT
-----------------------
• Real Implementation – Each challenge has a City department champion ready to adopt solutions
• Open Source by Design – Legal pathways pre-cleared so the City can actually use winning solutions
• 6-Month Support – Winners get City staff mentorship plus micro-grants ($1K–$5K) for piloting
• Built for Everyone – Welcomes designers, data analysts, policy experts, and community organizers—not just coders
• 10-Year Vision – Intended to become an annual Richmond tradition

STAKEHOLDER ECOSYSTEM
---------------------
Each track is supported by a Track Team consisting of:
• City Partner (government official/appointee)
• Corporate Partner (business sponsor providing bounty)
• Community Partner (nonprofit with domain expertise)

It's essentially a bridge between Richmond's tech talent and the mayor's 
civic priorities, designed to produce real, deployable solutions rather 
than just demos.
